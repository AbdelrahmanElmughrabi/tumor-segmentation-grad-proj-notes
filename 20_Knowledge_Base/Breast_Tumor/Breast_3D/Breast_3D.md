## General Notes
3D breast imaging provides volumetric context that 2D misses — overlapping tissue structures are resolved, tumor morphology is captured in full. However computational requirements are significantly higher.

Three distinct 3D modalities exist for breast imaging, each with different clinical use cases and technical challenges:
- DBT: 3D reconstruction from mammography X-rays
- DCE-MRI: most sensitive modality, used for high-risk screening
- ABUS: automated 3D ultrasound, standardized alternative to handheld
